Welcome

Welcome to our site that supports families in how young children develop speaking and listening skills. Stages for language development, Critical period hypothesis. Children acquire language beginning at birth, taking in the people and those around them on a daily basis. Simple activities — talking, listening, reading and playing together — can have a huge impact on how children learn to express themselves and understand others. This is a website explaining in child-friendly language how to understand the process of development of children’s communication skills over time, and practical strategies that family members can apply at home. We believe that children are capable, and every family brings knowledge, culture, and language to their child’s learning experience. This website supports inclusive and respectful approaches to developing communication much, whether your child is learning one language an multiple languages. Let’s work together to help children develop and nurture into confident communicators.

How Families Can Support Language

  • Talk to your child in new ways every day.
  • Read books with your child every day.
  • Everyday sing rhymes with your child and do these things daily
  • Integrate daily routines like playing, eating, bathing, etc. into opportunities for language development.
